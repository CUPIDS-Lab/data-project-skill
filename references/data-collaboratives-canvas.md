# Data Collaboratives Canvas

A structured canvas for designing multi-partner, cross-sector data collaborations deliberately rather than discovering the hard parts painfully. Source: https://datacollaboratives.org/canvas.html

## In one paragraph

The canvas walks a collaboration through phases — demand (problem, value), supply (capacity, data inventory and gaps), collaboration (partner selection and incentives), design (collaborative type, risks, mitigation, terms, governance), implementation (roles, resources), communication, and learning (baseline, metrics, impact). It is the strategic complement to the operational collaboration practices, and it drives the data-collaborative-canvas template at L3/L5.

## Key practices (sampleable units)

- **Define problem and value first** [collaborative, transparent] — a one-page problem statement (who benefits, why now) and a concrete value proposition before any data moves.
- **Inventory data supply and gaps** [documented] — what data exists internally/externally, and what's missing.
- **Match partners to incentives** [collaborative, inclusive] — map partners and their motivations; keep a level playing field for smaller partners.
- **Assess risks across the lifecycle** [transparent, inclusive] — collection, analysis, sharing, and use risks; prioritize by likelihood×severity; plan mitigation and monitoring.
- **Establish governance** [collaborative, transparent] — decision-making process, feedback loops, conflict resolution, and clear terms (liability, IP, ownership, public-release expectations).
- **Define baseline and impact metrics** [transparent] — measure progress against agreed indicators; iterate.

## When most relevant

Multi-organization or cross-sector collaborations, data-sharing agreements, partner recruitment. Strong signal on "partners," "data sharing," "collaborative," "cross-sector." L3 and L5.

## Caveats / conflicts

Strategic and fairly heavy; for a single-team internal project most blocks collapse to a few lines. Pair it with ProPublica Collaborative (roadmap) for the operational partner-management detail.
